Week 1 Grammar

Main target: subject pronouns + être. We start with useful sentence patterns, not long grammar explanations.

1. Main grammar target

Être means to be. In Week 1, you use it to say who someone is.

PronounÊtre formMeaning
jesuisI am
tuesyou are (informal singular)
ilesthe is
elleestshe is
noussommeswe are
vousêtesyou are (formal/plural)
ils / ellessontthey are

2. Review grammar target

None yet. This week, review means repeating the same core patterns many times with different names and days.

Memory reminder: Week 1 review happens inside the week itself. You see the same structures in vocabulary, reading, listening, writing, and the quiz.

3. Pattern ladder

Step 1: Name pattern

Je suis Alex.

Why this works

Je means I. suis is the form of être used with je.

Step 2: Ask informally

Tu es Samira.

Why this works

Tu is the informal singular you. It uses es.

Step 3: Talk about another person

Il est Marc.

Why this works

Il means he. With il, use est.

Step 4: Talk about a woman

Elle est Madame Dupont.

Why this works

Elle means she. It also uses est.

Step 5: Talk about a group

Nous sommes en classe.

Why this works

Nous means we. The form is sommes.

Step 6: Be polite or plural

Vous êtes Madame Dupont.

Why this works

Vous can mean one person politely or more than one person. The form is êtes.

4. Compare and notice

Je suis / Tu es

Je suis Alex.
I am Alex.

Tu es Samira.
You are Samira.

Il est / Elle est

Il est Marc.
He is Marc.

Elle est Madame Dupont.
She is Madame Dupont.

Watch out: do not say je est or tu suis. Keep the pronoun and the correct form together:
  • je → suis
  • tu → es
  • il / elle → est
  • nous → sommes
  • vous → êtes
Connector note: Early Week 1 keeps connectors very light. You mainly build one short sentence at a time.

5. Mini production prompts

  1. Write: “I am Alex.”
  2. Write: “He is Marc.”
  3. Write: “We are in class.”
  4. Write one sentence with bonjour and one sentence with merci.
Reveal possible answers
  • Je suis Alex.
  • Il est Marc.
  • Nous sommes en classe.
  • Bonjour, je suis Alex. / Merci, Madame Dupont.
