Ma ville

This passage uses blue for masculine articles and pink for feminine articles — the same rule as vocabulary and grammar. It also uses verbs from Week 2 (est, a, va, fait) so you can see articles and verbs working together.

le · la · l' · un · une = article (masculin/féminin)
est · a · va · fait = verb from Week 2
Ma ville
~120 mots · Niveau A2
Je m'appelle Sophie. J'habite dans une ville au Québec. Ma ville a un parc, une école, un restaurant et un café. Le parc est grand et beau. L'école est près de ma maison. Le matin, mon ami va au café. Il fait du pain et du fromage pour le repas. La rue principale a des magasins et une boulangerie. Le soir, ma famille est à la maison. Mon père fait la cuisine. Ma mère a un livre. C'est une bonne ville parce qu'elle est calme et belle.
My name is Sophie. I live in a city in Quebec. My city has a park, a school, a restaurant and a café. The park is big and beautiful. The school is near my house. In the morning, my friend goes to the café. He makes (some) bread and (some) cheese for the meal. The main street has (some) stores and a bakery. In the evening, my family is at home. My father cooks. My mother has a book. It is a good city because it is calm and beautiful.
Phrase par phrase

Read each sentence carefully. Notice how the article colour tells you the gender of every noun. Green = verb from Week 2.

Phrase 1
Je m'appelle Sophie.
zhuh ma-PEL so-FEE
My name is Sophie.
You learned Je m'appelle in Week 1 as a greeting and introduction. No article needed here — we are using a proper name.
Phrase 2
J'habite dans une ville au Québec.
zha-BEET dahn ün VEEL oh kay-BEK
I live in a city in Quebec.
une ville — indefinite feminine article. We use une because this is the first mention of "a city" (unspecified). au Québec = à + le Québec. Province names are masculine → au.
Phrase 3
Ma ville a un parc, une école, un restaurant et un café.
ma VEEL ah AN PARK, ün nay-KOL, AN res-toh-RAHN ay AN ka-FAY 🔗 une école → /ün-nay-KOL/
My city has a park, a school, a restaurant and a café.
a = elle a (avoir, Week 2). Look at the articles: un parc (masc), une école (fem), un restaurant (masc), un café (masc). Each one is indefinite because these are being introduced for the first time.
Phrase 4
Le parc est grand et beau.
luh PARK eh GRAHN ay BOH
The park is big and beautiful.
Now we use le parc (definite) — the park we just mentioned in the previous sentence. Article switches from indefinite (un) to definite (le) because both speaker and listener now know which park we mean. est = il est (être, Week 2).
Phrase 5
L'école est près de ma maison.
lay-KOL eh PREH duh ma may-ZOHN
The school is near my house.
L'école — definite, feminine, elision (vowel start). Previously we said une école; now it is known → l'école. Connector: près de = near/close to. No gender agreement needed on prepositions.
Phrase 6
Le matin, mon ami va au café.
luh ma-TAN, mohn na-MEE vah oh ka-FAY 🔗 mon ami → /mohn-na-MEE/
In the morning, my friend goes to the café.
va = il va (aller, Week 2). au = à + le (mandatory contraction — you cannot say "à le café"). Le matin as a time expression = "in the morning" (habitual).
Phrase 7
Il fait du pain et du fromage pour le repas.
eel feh dü PAN ay dü froh-MAZH poor luh ruh-PAH
He makes (some) bread and (some) cheese for the meal.
fait = il fait (faire, Week 2). du = de + le — the partitive article, meaning "some" of an uncountable thing. Du pain = some bread (not a specific loaf). Du fromage = some cheese.
Phrase 8
La rue principale a des magasins et une boulangerie.
la RÜ pran-see-PAL ah day ma-ga-ZAN ay ün boo-lahn-zhuh-REE
The main street has (some) stores and a bakery.
Phrase 9
Le soir, ma famille est à la maison.
luh SWAR, ma fa-MEE-yuh eh-tah la may-ZOHN
In the evening, my family is at home.
Phrase 10
Mon père fait la cuisine. Ma mère a un livre.
mohn PAIR feh la kwee-ZEEN. ma MAIR ah AN lee-vruh.
My father cooks. My mother has a book.
faire la cuisine is an idiomatic expression meaning "to cook" (literally "to do the cooking"). La cuisine is feminine. a = elle a (avoir). un livre — indefinite, because we don't know which specific book.
Phrase 11
C'est une bonne ville parce qu'elle est calme et belle.
seh ün BON VEEL par-suh kel eh KALM ay BEL
It is a good city because it is calm and beautiful.
parce que = because (connector). c'est une… — after c'est, use indefinite article + noun when describing with a quality: c'est une bonne ville. Adjectives bonne and belle are feminine to agree with ville (f.).
