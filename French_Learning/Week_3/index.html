Cette semaine

How gender works in French — and why it changes everything

Every French noun is either masculine or feminine. There is no neutral gender. The article attached to a noun — le, la, un, une — is the clearest signal of its gender, and it must match. This week you learn 40 essential nouns grouped by topic, and the 7 articles you need to use them correctly.

La règle de couleur — la même partout cette semaine

Every time you see a highlighted word in vocabulary, grammar, or the reading passage, it is always an article — never a noun, adjective, or anything else. The color tells you the gender of the noun that follows.

le · l' · un
Masculin
le livre · l'homme · un café
la · l' · une
Féminin
la maison · l'école · une pomme
💡 Élision: Before a vowel or silent h, both le and la shorten to l'. The gender is still there — you just have to know the noun. Example: l'école is feminine, l'homme is masculine.
