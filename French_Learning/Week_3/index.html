Cette semaine

Every French noun has a gender — masculine or feminine — and the article you use must match it. Master le, la, l' and les (the) plus un, une and des (a / some), and learn 50 essential nouns with their genders.

🎯 Objectifs de la semaine

  • ✅ Know the gender (masculine / feminine) of 50 common nouns
  • ✅ Use definite articles: le, la, l', les
  • ✅ Use indefinite articles: un, une, des
  • ✅ Understand elision — le/la → l' before a vowel or silent h
  • ✅ Form basic plurals with les + noun + -s
  • ✅ Recognize common masculine and feminine word endings

🔑 Le concept clé

In French there is no neutral gender — every noun is either masculine or feminine. The article (le or la) is the quickest signal of gender, so always learn a new noun together with its article.

Masculin
le / un
le livre · un café
Féminin
la / une
la table · une voiture
Elision — l'
Before a vowel or silent h, le and la both shorten to l':
l'homme  ·  l'école  ·  l'eau  ·  l'ami
