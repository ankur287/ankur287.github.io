Grammar: 4 Essential Verbs

être (to be) · avoir (to have) · aller (to go) · faire (to do/make)

What is conjugation?

In English, verbs mostly stay the same: "I go", "you go", "she goes". Only the third person adds an -s.

In French, verbs change their ending for every subject pronoun. This is called conjugation. The four verbs this week are all irregular — meaning their forms don't follow a pattern and must be memorised.

The tables below show: who is doing the action (subject), what the French word becomes, how to say it, and what it means in English.

📌 Subject Pronouns — Quick Reference

These are the "who" words. Every conjugation below uses one of these.

French pronoun English meaning When to use it
je (j') I Talking about yourself. Becomes j' before a vowel.
tu you (informal) To a friend, family member, or child.
il / elle / on he / she / one (or we, informally) il = he, elle = she. On is used informally to mean 'we'.
nous we Formal 'we'. In spoken French, people often say 'on' instead.
vous you (formal or plural) To a stranger, boss — or to multiple people at once.
ils / elles they ils = group with ≥1 male. elles = all-female group.
1. être
= to be

Used to describe who someone is, their job, nationality, location, or characteristics.

Subject (who) French form How to say it English meaning
je
= I
 suis SWEE I am
tu
= you (friend)
 es EH you are
il / elle / on
= he / she / one
 est EH he/she is
nous
= we
 sommes SUM we are
vous
= you (formal/group)
 êtes EHT you are
ils / elles
= they
 sont SON they are

Gender agreement with être: Adjectives after être must match the subject's gender. "Il est content" (he is happy) but "Elle est contente" (she is happy — add -e for feminine).

On = nous in spoken French: "On est canadiens" = "Nous sommes canadiens" (We are Canadian). On is very common in informal speech.

Liaison: "Vous êtes" → pronounced "voo-ZET". The s of vous links to the vowel of êtes. This linking of sounds is called liaison.

2. avoir
= to have

Used for possession AND for many body/emotional states that English expresses with 'to be'.

Subject (who) French form How to say it English meaning
je → j'
= I
 j'ai ZHAY I have
tu
= you (friend)
 as AH you have
il / elle / on
= he / she / one
 a AH he/she has
nous
= we
 avons za-VON we have
vous
= you (formal/group)
 avez za-VAY you have
ils / elles
= they
 ont ZON they have

⚠️ avoir ≠ être for states

English uses to be for these. French uses avoir (to have):
J'ai faim = I have hunger = I am hungry
J'ai soif = I have thirst = I am thirsty
J'ai vingt ans = I have twenty years = I am twenty years old
J'ai raison = I have reason = I am right

Elision: When je comes before a word starting with a vowel or silent h, the e of je drops and an apostrophe is added. "Je ai" → "j'ai". This happens with all vowel-initial words: j'habite, j'aime, j'ai.

Liaison: "Nous avons" → "noo-ZA-von". "Ils ont" → "eel-ZON". The final consonant of the subject pronoun links to the vowel starting the verb.

3. aller
= to go

Used for movement AND to express the near future (going to do something).

Subject (who) French form How to say it English meaning
je
= I
 vais VAY I go / I am going
tu
= you (friend)
 vas VAH you go
il / elle / on
= he / she / one
 va VAH he/she goes
nous
= we
 allons za-LON we go
vous
= you (formal/group)
 allez za-LAY you go
ils / elles
= they
 vont VON they go

✅ Near future: aller + infinitive verb

Aller + any infinitive verb = going to do something:
Je vais manger. = I am going to eat.
Elle va partir. = She is going to leave.
Nous allons travailler. = We are going to work.

Use à after aller: "Je vais à l'école" = I go to school. À = to (a place).

Contraction — à + le = au: You CANNOT say "je vais à le cinéma". It MUST be "je vais au cinéma". À + le always contracts to au.

Casual greeting: "Ça va ?" = Are you OK? (lit. 'it goes?'). Response: "Ça va bien" = I'm doing well.

4. faire
= to do / to make

Used for activities, chores, sports, and many fixed phrases. French often uses faire where English uses a specific verb (cook, exercise, walk).

Subject (who) French form How to say it English meaning
je
= I
 fais FAY I do / I make
tu
= you (friend)
 fais FAY you do / you make
il / elle / on
= he / she / one
 fait FAY he/she does
nous
= we
 faisons ⚠️ fuh-ZON fuh-ZON we do / we make
vous
= you (formal/group)
 faites ⚠️ irregular FET you do / you make
ils / elles
= they
 font FON they do / they make

⚠️ Trap 1 — Nous faisons: Despite the spelling fai-, the vowel sound changes to "fuh" (like the e in le). Say: "noo-fuh-ZON", not "noo-FAY-zon".

⚠️ Trap 2 — Vous faites: Most verbs use -ez for vous (vous parlez, vous mangez). Faites is an irregular exception — say "FET".

Common faire expressions:
faire la cuisine → to cook (lit. to do the cooking)
faire les courses → to go grocery shopping
faire du sport → to exercise
faire une promenade → to go for a walk
faire attention → to pay attention
il fait beau → the weather is nice (not elle/je — weather uses il)
il fait froid → it is cold (weather)

Practice Sentences

Nous sommes étudiants et nous faisons nos devoirs tous les soirs.
We are students and we do our homework every evening.
Nous sommes = we are (être, nous form)  |  étudiants = students  |  et = and (connector)  |  nous faisons = we do (faire, nous form — pronounced fuh-ZON)  |  nos devoirs = our homework  |  tous les soirs = every evening
Elle a faim parce qu'elle va souvent à la gym.
She is hungry because she often goes to the gym.
Elle a faim = she has hunger = she is hungry (avoir faim idiom)  |  parce qu' = because (connector; qu' before vowel)  |  elle va = she goes (aller, elle form)  |  souvent = often  |  à la gym = to the gym
Tu es à la maison maintenant ou tu vas au bureau ?
Are you at home now or are you going to the office?
Tu es = you are (être, tu form)  |  à la maison = at home  |  maintenant = now  |  ou = or (connector)  |  tu vas = you go (aller, tu form)  |  au bureau = to the office (au = à + le)
Ils font la cuisine ensemble et ils ont toujours de bons repas.
They cook together and they always have good meals.
Ils font = they do/make (faire, ils form)  |  la cuisine = the cooking (faire la cuisine = to cook)  |  ensemble = together  |  et = and  |  ils ont = they have (avoir, ils form — liaison: "eel-ZON")  |  toujours = always  |  de bons repas = good meals
J'ai besoin d'aide parce que je fais une erreur.
I need help because I am making a mistake.
J'ai besoin = I have need = I need (avoir besoin idiom)  |  d'aide = of help (de + aide; d' before vowel)  |  parce que = because (subordinating connector)  |  je fais = I do/make (faire, je form)  |  une erreur = a mistake (erreur = feminine noun → une, not un)
