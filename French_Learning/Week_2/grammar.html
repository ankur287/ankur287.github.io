Grammar: 4 Essential Verbs

être · avoir · aller · faire — 3 spoken examples per conjugation so you can hear how sounds blend.

What is conjugation?

In English, most verbs barely change: "I go, you go, she goes." In French, verbs change their ending for every subject pronoun. The four verbs this week are all irregular — their forms don't follow a pattern and must be memorised.

Each pronoun block below shows the same 3 sentences so you can compare how the verb sounds in context — not just in isolation.

🔊 How to read the phonetics

Phonetics show how the full sentence sounds when spoken naturally. French words blend together — this is called liaison.

zh = s in "measure"  ·  ew = round lips for "oo" but say "ee"  ·  ON = nasal (bon)  ·  AN = nasal (encore)  ·  -Z- or -T- between words = liaison sound blending two words together

Example: il est étudiant — the t in est links to étudiant → eel-EH-tay-tew-DYON. Without liaison you'd expect a gap — there isn't one.

Practice Sentences

All four verbs mixed together
