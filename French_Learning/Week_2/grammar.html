Grammar: 4 Essential Verbs

être · avoir · aller · faire — with a full example sentence for every conjugation.

What is conjugation?

In English, most verbs barely change: "I go, you go, she goes." In French, verbs change their ending for every subject pronoun. This week's four verbs are all irregular — their forms don't follow a regular pattern and must be memorised.

Each row in the tables below shows the same sentence pattern for every subject, so you can hear and see exactly how the verb changes.

Practice Sentences

Tap "Show Translation" to check your understanding

Nous sommes étudiants et nous faisons nos devoirs tous les soirs.
We are students and we do our homework every evening.
Nous sommes = we are (être)  |  étudiants = students  |  et = and  |  nous faisons = we do (faire — pronounced fuh-ZON)  |  nos devoirs = our homework  |  tous les soirs = every evening
Elle a faim parce qu'elle va souvent à la gym.
She is hungry because she often goes to the gym.
Elle a faim = she has hunger = she is hungry (avoir faim idiom)  |  parce qu' = because  |  elle va = she goes (aller)  |  souvent = often  |  à la gym = to the gym
Tu es à la maison maintenant ou tu vas au bureau ?
Are you at home now or are you going to the office?
Tu es = you are (être)  |  à la maison = at home  |  maintenant = now  |  ou = or  |  tu vas = you go (aller)  |  au bureau = to the office (au = à + le)
Ils font la cuisine ensemble et ils ont toujours de bons repas.
They cook together and they always have good meals.
Ils font = they do/make (faire)  |  la cuisine = the cooking  |  ensemble = together  |  et = and  |  ils ont = they have (avoir — liaison: eel-ZON)  |  toujours = always  |  de bons repas = good meals
