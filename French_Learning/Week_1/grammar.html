Greetings Structure

Learn how to choose the right greeting, introduce yourself confidently, and understand key patterns for numbers, days, and months.

📐 Rule 1 — Formal vs Informal Greetings

French has two levels of politeness. Use formal (vous) with strangers, elders, and professionals. Use informal (tu) with friends, family, and children.

Situation Formal (vous) Informal (tu)
Greeting Bonjour / Bonsoir Salut
How are you? Comment allez-vous ? Ça va ?
Your name? Comment vous appelez-vous ? Comment tu t'appelles ?
Goodbye Au revoir Salut / À bientôt
Bonjour, madame. Comment allez-vous ?
Good morning, ma'am. How are you?
Salut, ça va ? Oui, ça va très bien !
Hey, how's it going? Yeah, going great!
📐 Rule 2 — Introducing Yourself

Use these three key phrases to introduce yourself in French. Note how the verb changes based on the subject.

French Phrase English Example
Je m'appelle + [name] My name is… Je m'appelle Marie.
J'ai + [number] + ans I am … years old J'ai vingt-cinq ans.
Je suis + [nationality/job] I am (a)… Je suis canadien(ne).
J'habite à + [city] I live in… J'habite à Montréal.
Je m'appelle Thomas. J'ai trente ans. Je suis canadien et j'habite à Ottawa.
My name is Thomas. I am thirty years old. I am Canadian and I live in Ottawa.
Bonjour ! Je m'appelle Aisha. Je suis médecin. J'habite à Montréal depuis deux ans.
Hello! My name is Aisha. I am a doctor. I have lived in Montreal for two years.

📐 Rule 4 — Days of the Week

Days of the week in French are not capitalized. Use le + [day] to express a recurring event ("on Mondays"). Without le, it refers to a specific upcoming day.

Le lundi, je vais à la salle de sport.
On Mondays (every Monday), I go to the gym.
Lundi, j'ai rendez-vous avec le médecin.
This Monday, I have a doctor's appointment.
📐 Rule 5 — Months & Dates

Months in French are also not capitalized. Use en + [month] to say "in [month]". For dates, use the structure: le [number] [month].

Mon anniversaire est en janvier, le quinze.
My birthday is in January, on the fifteenth.
En février, il neige souvent à Montréal.
In February, it often snows in Montreal.
