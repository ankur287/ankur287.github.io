🔢 Tens, Hundreds & Compound Numbers
💡 How French big numbers work:
Seventy = soixante-dix (sixty-ten). Eighty = quatre-vingts (four-twenties). Ninety = quatre-vingt-dix (four-twenty-ten). These are quirky but essential — you'll see them on prices, addresses, phone numbers, and transit signs.
Vingt
VAHN
Twenty
Il y a vingt étudiants dans la classe.
There are twenty students in the class.
Trente
TRAHNT
Thirty
Mon frère a trente ans cette année.
My brother is thirty years old this year.
Quarante
kah-RAHNT
Forty
Le trajet dure quarante minutes en autobus.
The journey takes forty minutes by bus.
Cinquante
sahn-KAHNT
Fifty
Le billet coûte cinquante dollars canadiens.
The ticket costs fifty Canadian dollars.
Soixante
swah-SAHNT
Sixty
Une heure a soixante minutes.
One hour has sixty minutes.
Soixante-dix
swah-SAHNT-DEES
Seventy (lit. sixty-ten)
Ma grand-mère a soixante-dix ans.
My grandmother is seventy years old.
Quatre-vingts
kah-truh-VAHN
Eighty (lit. four-twenties)
Le immeuble a quatre-vingts appartements.
The building has eighty apartments.
Quatre-vingt-dix
kah-truh-vahn-DEES
Ninety (lit. four-twenty-ten)
Il y a quatre-vingt-dix jours dans ce trimestre.
There are ninety days in this term.
Cent
SAHN
One Hundred
Il y a cent centimes dans un dollar.
There are one hundred cents in a dollar.
🔢 Compound Numbers in Context
💡 How to build compound numbers:
Add a hyphen between the tens and the unit: quatre-vingt-treize (93). For hundreds, say cent then the rest: cent sept (107). For hundreds + tens: six cent quatre (604).
Quatre-vingt-treize
kah-truh-vahn-TREH-z
93 — Ninety-three
Mon grand-père a quatre-vingt-treize ans — il est en bonne santé.
My grandfather is ninety-three years old — he is in good health.
Cent sept
sahn SET
107 — One hundred and seven
Il y a cent sept étudiants inscrits au programme de français.
There are one hundred and seven students enrolled in the French program.
Six cent quatre
see sahn KAH-truh
604 — Six hundred and four
Mon adresse est au six cent quatre, rue Saint-Denis, à Montréal.
My address is 604 Saint-Denis Street, in Montreal.
